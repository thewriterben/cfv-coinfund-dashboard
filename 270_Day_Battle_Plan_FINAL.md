## FUND ECONOMICS: THE 80/20 STRUCTURE
## Capital Allocation
Each of the three CoinFunds splits every dollar raised into two pools: 80% is deployed into the 12
coins in equal amounts and locked in a multi-signature wallet controlled by the fund. 20% is the
management  pool,  covering  team  operations,  exchange  listings,  wallet  and  POS  integration
development,  Merkle  tree  audit  compliance,  and  ecosystem  grants. This  mirrors  the  standard  2-
and-20 venture fund structure calculated over a ten-year fund life (2% annual management fee x
10 years = 20%).
Token Mechanics (Chia Blockchain)
Issuance:  Each  dollar  of  committed  capital  is  represented  by  one  token  issued  on  the  Chia
blockchain. Fund I issues up to 1 billion tokens. Fund II: 5 billion. Fund III: 15 billion.
Trading: Tokens may only be traded on the Chia blockchain and only between verified accredited
investors. No public exchange trading. No transfer to non-accredited parties. Tokens continue to
exist and trade indefinitely.
First  Trade  —  Management  Fee  Recovery:  When  a  token  is  sold  for  the  first  time,  the  20%
management fee must be recovered before any profit is calculated. If an investor bought tokens
at $1.00 each and sells at $1.50, the first $0.20 covers the management fee. The remaining $0.30
of profit is subject to 20% carried interest ($0.06 to fund manager). Net to seller: $1.24.
Years 1–10 Carried Interest: 20%. Every trade of a token during the first ten years pays 20% of
any   profit   to   the   fund   manager.   This   is   automatically   calculated   and   collected   through
mechanisms embedded in the Chia blockchain smart contract.
Year 11+ Carried Interest: 2.1%. Beginning in Year 11, the carried interest drops to 2.1% of profit
on each trade and remains at that level indefinitely. This provides permanent, sustainable revenue
to the fund manager while dramatically reducing the cost of trading for token holders.
COINS  ARE  LOCKED  FOREVER:  The  coins  acquired  by  each  fund  are  held  permanently  in
multi-signature  wallets  controlled  by  the  fund.  They  are  never  sold.  They  are  never  distributed.
They  are  never  redeemed. The  coins  remain  locked  indefinitely,  creating  permanent  scarcity  for
all  12  coins.  This  eliminates  the  dumping  risk  that  destroys  value  in  funds  that  distribute
underlying  assets.  The  tokens  represent  a  permanent,  tradeable  claim  on  locked  coins  whose
scarcity only increases over time.
No  Fund  Dissolution:  Unlike  traditional  venture  funds  with  10-year  fund  lives,  the  CoinFunds  do

in the bill, but it validates the DGSB and CFV used with DGD and ZCL.
